You know the moment. Someone pulls up the karaoke song list and the group goes quiet. Not because nobody wants to sing. Because nobody can agree on a song they can all handle. One person is a baritone. Another barely leaves their chest voice. Someone else only knows Taylor Swift. And the one friend who actually sings well just picks something nobody else can follow.

Picking group karaoke songs has always been a guessing game. You scroll through a list, someone throws out "Bohemian Rhapsody," and three people quietly know they cannot hit a single note in the chorus. The song gets picked anyway. Half the group mumbles through it. Nobody sounds great.

That problem is why we built SquadMatch.

What Is SquadMatch?

SquadMatch is a new feature in HumMatch that analyzes every voice in your group and finds songs that everyone can actually sing together. Not just songs where the ranges overlap on paper, but songs where the vocal character of each person in the group fits the track.

Think of it as HumMatch for groups. Each person hums three notes, SquadMatch builds a composite vocal profile for the whole squad, and then it returns a playlist of songs that work for everyone. Not compromises. Not "close enough." Songs your group will genuinely sound good on.

"Solo singing is great. But the best karaoke moments happen when the whole room is in it together."

Why This Matters

Most karaoke apps treat singing as a solo activity. You find your songs, you sing your songs. But real life is not like that. Karaoke is social. Road trips are social. Pregames are social. The moments people remember are the ones where the whole group was singing together and it actually sounded good.

The problem is that finding songs for multiple voices is genuinely hard. Every person has a different range, a different tone, and a different comfort zone. What works for a tenor does not work for an alto. What works for a soprano does not work for a baritone. And "just pick something everyone knows" ignores the fact that knowing a song and being able to sing it are two completely different things.

SquadMatch solves this by building a group vocal profile that accounts for every member. It does not just find the lowest common denominator. It finds songs where each voice in the group has something to contribute.

How It Works

1
Squad Leader creates a session. Open SquadMatch from your dashboard, name your squad, and share the invite link with your group. No app downloads required. Everyone joins from their phone's browser.
2
Everyone hums three notes. Each member records a low, mid, and high hum. Takes about 30 seconds per person. HumMatch analyzes each voice individually and builds a composite group profile from the combined data.
3
Get your group playlist. SquadMatch returns songs ranked by how well they fit the whole group. Filter by genre, decade, or vibe. Export the list and you have a ready-made group karaoke playlist that everyone can actually sing.

The key difference: SquadMatch does not just look for overlapping ranges. It uses the same spectral analysis that powers individual HumMatch results to find songs where the combined tonal character of your group fits the track. A group of three baritones gets different results than a mixed group with a soprano, a tenor, and an alto. That is the whole point.
